Capture the moment

Tamron 28 - 75 mm: My workhorse, it allows me to shoot almost everything that comes across a camera lens. Powerful enough to create sharp pictures, also in low-light conditions, it is also a lens that allows you to shoot portraits.

Tamron 70 - 300 mm: A telephoto zoom lens that I use for all work up to 200 or 300 meters. It is surprisingly sharp for a zoom of that size and price and it was the lens that I used for the horse head in the gallery.

Tamron 150 - 600: The "big gun" for the extreme things. Nicknamed "Monster" it weighs 2 kilos and guarantees a sore neck when you carry the camera with it around your neck for only a few minutes. Only a shoulder strap can help there, or an assistant...

Samyang 24 PC: A tilt-shift lens especially for architecture and for all other shots where you want or nee to change the perspective. This lens can be twisted, so it is no autofocus lens.

Shoulder strap: It can easily carry a few kilos (camera and the 150 - 600 are together around 3 kilos, incl. battery grip). As the strap sits on your shoulder and then goes across your chest, it spreads the weight evenly.

Monopod: A "one-legged" tripod, mainly used to provide more stability and in light conditions where your need some support. It also makes hiking more comfortable.

Tripod: The only way to get shots without camera shake (with an additional remote control). I use it for everthing that requires a long time, e.g. landscapes, low-light, night, etc.
